Blog Post 10: Week 13

How our code matters as developers in the context of decolonialism, and identifying things in our own coding practice that we must be aware of.

An ethical internet should be one that consists of inclusivity, transparency, privacy and sustainability, prioritizing human dignity and digital self-determination. Central to this vision is the commitment to universal access, where web technologies are designed to be inclusive across diverse socioeconomic and ability spectrums (W3C, 2021). Users should be informed about how their data is used, how algorithms function and who is responsible for content moderation. It keeps privacy through frameworks like “Privacy by Design” which advocates proactive, user-centric data governance (Cavoukian, 2021). This involves collecting only necessary data and properly communicating about that data usage and giving the user control over their personal information. Transparency and accountability in algorithmic systems and data practices are essential to foster user trust and digital literacy and ethical internet should always support healthy online communities. This can be achieved through mitigating harmful behaviours and misinformation while preserving freedom of expression (W3C, 2021). The internet should support constructive dialogue and community building but also have measures to prevent harmful acts such as harassment or any online abuse. An ethical design of a website means to create user interfaces that are intuitive and one that respects user autonomy. This involves eliminating manipulative interface patterns and ensuring informed user content (Gray et al., 2018). Users should be able to have agency over their digital experience which involves a participatory design of the digital environment where communities can actively engage in creating digital solutions that reflect their values and needs. Environmental sustainability is increasingly recognised, calling for digital systems that minimise energy use and electronic waste (W3C, 2021). The environmental impact of digital technologies cannot be overlooked. It is essential that in web development, developers consider energy consumption, electronic waste and how to promote sustainable practices. An ethical internet aligns with the principles of equity, agency and ecological responsibility, requiring ongoing collaborative stewardship by technologists, policymakers and users. It also requires continuous effort and vigilance to ensure that digital spaces remain inclusive, respectful and empowering for all users.
