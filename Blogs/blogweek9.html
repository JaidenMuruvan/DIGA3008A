Blog Post 6: Week 9

What Is Digital Equality

In the article “What is digital equality?” an interview with Nanjira Sambuli discussed the multifaceted nature of digital equality. She emphasizes that digital equality involves not just providing access to technology but also addressing historical and systemic inequalities that influence how different communities engage with digital tools. Sambuli outlines the importance of inclusive and policy making that considers these complexities to ensure that digital advancements benefit all segments of society, aligning with the Sustainable Development Goals’ commitment to “leave no one behind.”

Digital Divide Widens

In the article “Digital Divide Widens” by Maria Takati and Dimitris Glynos it examines how the pandemic of COVID – 19 has intensified existing digital inequalities, particularly in education and employment. For educational disparities over 1.5 billion student have been faced with school closures due to the pandemic. Approximately 830 million lacked access to a computer, and 40% of the student had no internet at home, and many of these student was not able to access remote learning alternatives. This digital divide affected students in low-income countries and marginalized communities, with 82% of students in Sub – Saharan Africa unable to go online. Employment inequities was also a topic in this article as the shift to teleworking benefited mainly high-income individuals whose jobs allowed them too work remotely or known today as “work from home.” In the U.S. only 29% of workers could work from home leaving the majority, especially those in lower-income brackets unable to do so.
